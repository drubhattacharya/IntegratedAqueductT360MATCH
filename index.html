Foundation 1 of 6 · Architecture

The Four-Layer Value Model

Before calculating a single dollar, you need a mental model of how NEMT creates financial value. Every layer builds on the one below. Confidence — and risk — increases as you move up the stack.

🔑
Core Principle: NEMT is not a standalone revenue generator. It is an enabler that makes other, already-established care management programs more effective. The financial case is built layer by layer, and your board recommendation must honor the distinction between layers that are directly traceable and layers that require additional evidence.
Click each layer to expand the rationale and attribution confidence
0
FFS Contribution Margin
Recovered revenue from prevented no-shows
HIGH Confidence
What it is: Each NEMT-enabled kept appointment generates net revenue equal to the visit's allowed amount minus marginal clinical cost. This is the program's financial floor — not ceiling.

Why it's high confidence: The causal chain is short and directly traceable: ride completed → appointment kept → claim generated → revenue collected. No intermediary attribution assumptions are required.

Evidence anchor: Shekelle et al. (2022) found a pooled 37% reduction in missed appointment odds (OR 0.63, 95% CI: 0.48–0.83) with HIGH certainty evidence for appointment adherence.1 CMS cites 25%+ of no-shows as transportation-attributable across primary care populations.3

Platform insight (T360 Phase I): Healthcare administrators consistently identified targeting the highest-cost no-show patients — by diagnosis or demographics — as essential to defensible ROI analysis. However, they found it prohibitively time-consuming to identify unit costs without standardized benchmarks. For example: the average hemodialysis patient misses 7.1 appointments per year, with only 9.8% rescheduled the next day, generating ~$1,500 in lost Medicare reimbursement per patient per year at the base rate of $232/appointment. Building specialty-specific benchmarks into the ROI calculator directly addresses this adoption barrier.16
1
Coding & Documentation Uplift
Z-code and CPT accuracy improvements
HIGH-MED Confidence
What it is: NEMT programs generate structured data about transportation barriers that can be coded (Z75.3 — Transportation barriers to healthcare) and documented in the EHR. This supports more accurate claim coding and risk adjustment, producing revenue uplift per encounter.

Why it's high-medium confidence: The coding uplift is real and measurable, but it requires operational workflow changes — staff must be trained to ask screening questions and document findings consistently. The revenue is predictable given workflow compliance.

Compliance note: CPT code content is copyright-protected by the AMA. Documentation tools that display CPT descriptors require an AMA license. Always reference CMS MLN materials instead of reproducing raw CPT text in internal tools.
2
CCM Enrollment Lift
Incremental Chronic Care Management billing attributable to NEMT
MEDIUM Confidence
What it is: Patients who keep NEMT-enabled appointments can be enrolled in Chronic Care Management (CCM). The incremental enrollment lift — above baseline CCM enrollment — is what's attributable to the NEMT program (typically ~40% of gross CCM revenue).

Why it's medium confidence: Not all enrolled CCM patients have transportation barriers, so only the incremental enrollment enabled by NEMT outreach is credited to the program. This requires a propensity-matched control group or A/B enrollment cohort to measure precisely.

Evidence anchor: TCM reduces 30-day readmissions by 22–32%5, with an 86.6% reduction in readmission odds in one study.13 ASPE found TCM decreases spending by 11% — yet only 17.9% of eligible beneficiaries received TCM in 2019.14
3
TCM Completion
Transitional Care Management directly enabled by NEMT transport
HIGH Confidence
What it is: TCM requires a face-to-face visit within 7 days (99496, high MDM) or 14 days (99495, moderate MDM) of discharge. NEMT directly and traceably enables that visit — without a ride, a high percentage of LMI patients miss the window and $201–$273 in TCM billing is forfeited per discharge.

Why it's HIGH confidence: Attribution is binary and auditable — the F2F visit either happened within the window or it didn't, and the NEMT trip log documents whether a ride was provided. No attribution model or cohort study required. This is the highest-confidence layer in the entire model after FFS base.

Workbook alignment: The Master Learner Workbook lists Layer 3 (TCM) as HIGH confidence separately from Layer 2 (CCM). The simulation treats these together in Exercise 3 but applies independent attribution logic to each.
4
VBC Quality Earn-back
Quality measure closure via MIPS & ACO REACH
LOW-MED Confidence
What it is: Kept appointments close quality measure gaps — HbA1c tests performed, blood pressure recorded, post-discharge visits completed. Under value-based contracts (MIPS, ACO REACH), quality score improvements translate to financial earn-back or avoided penalties.

Why it's low-medium confidence: Attribution is the field's weakest link. Social risks cluster (transportation, housing, food insecurity co-occur), NEMT is typically bundled with other interventions, and no published study has isolated NEMT's independent contribution to quality measure performance with high rigor.9 Your board presentation must include a planned attribution study.

Key evidence gap: No published studies combine NEMT with CCM/TCM programs. The Berkowitz ACO evaluation found increased outpatient visits but no reduction in admissions or ED visits — the program was not cost-saving.9
⚠️
The Attribution Trap: Each layer up the stack requires more assumptions and a longer causal chain. Presenting all four layers as equally certain to a board finance committee is the most common error in NEMT financial analysis — and the one most likely to produce a perceived Year 1 failure even when the program succeeds.

Critical Nuance
The Chaiyachati 2018 Contradiction

A JAMA Internal Medicine RCT of 786 Medicaid beneficiaries found complimentary Lyft rides offered universally produced no reduction in no-shows (36.5% vs. 36.7%). Only 26% of those offered rides used them.2

Critical Evidence Gaps
🚫
No published studies combine NEMT with CCM/TCM programs. While CMS designed these as complementary pathways, and NEMT logically enables both, no research has measured outcomes of bundled NEMT + CCM or NEMT + TCM interventions.
Berkowitz et al. 2022 — ACO NEMT Evaluation9

Medicare ACO NEMT program. Result: outpatient visits increased +9.2/person/year but no reduction in admissions or ED visits. Program was not cost-saving.

🔬
This is the most rigorous ACO-level evaluation. It does not undermine the case for NEMT but reframes it: NEMT alone, without bundled care management, generates utilization without downstream savings. The financial case requires the CCM/TCM layer to capture the downstream value.

The TAM evaluation found both perceived usefulness and ease-of-use scored below 3.0 (the feasibility threshold on a 7-point scale), with the strongest endorsement from managers and support staff — the same audience this module targets.

Key Compliance Rules — Click to Expand
CCM Patient Eligibility (MLN909188)▶
Two or more chronic conditions expected to last ≥12 months that place the patient at significant risk of death, decompensation, or functional decline. Written or verbal consent required (verbal permitted since CY2017 rule). A certified EHR must be used. An electronic comprehensive care plan must include: problem list, measurable treatment goals, symptom management, medication management, and coordination with outside resources. 24/7 access to clinical staff must be provided.
CCM Incident-to Supervision (42 CFR 410.26)▶
Clinical staff — RNs, LPNs, nurse care managers, medical assistants (per state scope), pharmacists, and social workers — may furnish CCM services under general supervision (physician need not be physically present). This is a significant departure from the standard incident-to direct supervision requirement. However, 99491 cannot be furnished incident-to (per CY2019 final rule) and must be personally provided by the billing practitioner. Complex CCM (99487/99489) requires the billing practitioner to personally perform moderate-to-high complexity MDM during the period.
Non-Duplication Rules — CCM and TCM in Same Month▶
CCM and TCM can be billed in the same calendar month under specific conditions: (1) the TCM 30-day service period must end before the month's end; (2) at least 20 minutes of qualifying CCM services must be provided after the TCM period ends; (3) all CCM requirements must be independently met. Time cannot be double-counted.

CCM cannot be billed concurrently with: home health supervision (G0181), hospice supervision (G0182), certain ESRD codes, or new APCM codes. Non-complex and complex CCM cannot be reported for the same patient in the same month.
The Cardinal Compliance Rule: What Time Is Billable?▶
Only genuine clinical staff time counts. Billable CCM time includes: patient assessments, care plan updates, medication reviews, coordination calls, secure messaging — activities requiring clinical judgment or care management skill.

NEVER billable: Patient travel time (in taxi, rideshare, or waiting for pickup). Clerical scheduling. Transportation coordinator logistics time. Billing the time spent arranging NEMT as CCM clinical time is a False Claims Act exposure.

NEMT's value to CCM is structural — it creates the clinical window (the appointment) during which real CCM work happens. The ride enables the service; it is never the service itself.
Foundation 4 of 6 · Compliance

The Compliance Architecture

NEMT programs operate at the intersection of four legal and regulatory frameworks. Violating any one of them can transform a mission-aligned program into a liability. The simulation includes scored decisions where knowing these rules is the only right answer.

AKS Local Transportation Safe Harbor — 42 CFR § 1001.952(bb)

The 2016 Final Rule (81 FR 88368), amended 2020 (85 FR 77684), created a safe harbor for individual NEMT arrangements. All conditions below must be met simultaneously. Click each card to reveal the rule.

🎓
Why Interactive Format? T360 Phase I end-user research found that healthcare administrators strongly preferred an interactive training module over a static compliance checklist. A static list was perceived as inadequate for staff who need to apply the rules to real program design decisions — the same insight driving this flip-card format.16 The compliance training developed from this research is designed for up to 1 hour CEU-eligible annual professional development.
①
Uniform Policy Required

②
No Volume-or-Value Linkage
Click to reveal the rule
The transportation benefit cannot be contingent on the volume or value of referrals. No "ride bonus" for patients who refer others or schedule additional procedures.
③

④

The transportation benefit cannot be publicly advertised to attract new patients. Outreach must be to established patients through clinical channels only.
⑤

Transportation is only for individuals who are already established patients of the entity. It cannot be used as an inducement to attract new Medicare or Medicaid patients.

The entity must bear all transportation costs without shifting them to federal programs. The health system or its partners pay — the bill cannot be submitted to Medicare/Medicaid.

Patient Engagement Safe Harbor (42 CFR § 1001.952(hh)): A separate safe harbor permits VBE (value-based enterprise) participants to furnish transportation as in-kind support, subject to a $500 annual per-patient cap. Useful for ACO participants offering transportation as a supplemental benefit beyond the local transportation safe harbor.
CPT Copyright — A Compliance Trap

CPT code descriptors are copyright-protected by the AMA. Building internal documentation tools, EHR templates, or training materials that reproduce CPT descriptors verbatim requires an AMA license.

✅
The Safe Path: Reference CMS MLN materials (federally licensed) as the primary source in all documentation tools. Use code numbers with links to the AMA lookup tool; do not reproduce the descriptor inline. AMA actively enforces copyright — "fair use" is not a defense for systematic reproduction in workflow tools.
IRS Schedule H — Community Benefit Reporting

For nonprofit hospitals, NEMT program costs are reported under:

✓
Part I, Line 7e — Community Health Improvement Services
Transportation explicitly listed as an example in CHA guidance. Counts toward community benefit total.
✓
Part I, Line 7i — In-kind Contributions to NEMT Organizations
Cash or in-kind support. Also counts toward community benefit total.
Part II, Line 7 — Community Building Activities
Does NOT count toward community benefit total unless evidence-based health improvement outcomes are demonstrated.
Foundation 5 of 6 · Framing

Attribution & Board Communication

The most sophisticated financial model means nothing if it's framed incorrectly to a board. Two disciplines separate rigorous executive presentations from advocacy documents: attribution methodology and scenario anchoring.

Part A — Attribution Confidence Framework

Every dollar in your model carries an implicit confidence level. Presenting all layers as equally certain is an analytical error. Here is how to frame each layer:

LayerAttribution MethodConfidenceWhat You Need for Higher Confidence
FFS ContributionDirect claim-level tracing: ride → appointment → claimHIGHNothing more — this is fully traceable today.
TCM BillingBinary event tracing: F2F within 7/14 days occurred?HIGHDocument which TCM visits were NEMT-enabled (add transport flag to EMR).
CCM Enrollment LiftIncremental attribution: % of CCM enrollment attributable to NEMT outreachMEDIUMA/B enrollment cohort or propensity-matched control group.
VBC Quality Earn-backMatched cohort or interrupted time series — isolating NEMT's contribution from concurrent interventionsLOW-MEDFormal attribution study (e.g., REP matched-cohort design). Cannot be presented as fully attributable without one.
🔬
The Recommended Attribution Architecture: Measure each link in the causal chain separately (ride completion → appointment show rate → clinical measurement obtained → quality numerator change). Use interrupted time series at the practice/ACO level. Employ dose-response analysis correlating ride volume with outcomes. Conduct sensitivity analysis using E-values or Rosenbaum bounds to account for unmeasured confounding. The National Academy of Medicine notes that social risks cluster — transportation, housing, and food insecurity co-occur — making isolation essential.
Part B — Board Communication Discipline
The Three-Scenario Framework

Never present a single number to a board finance committee. Present three scenarios anchored to confidence levels:

ScenarioLayers IncludedRationale
Conservative (Year 1 floor)FFS base + Coding onlyMinimum floor — even if CCM/TCM takes longer to implement
★ Base Case (Anchor)Conservative + confirmed TCMAvoids overpromising; only high-confidence, directly-traceable layers
Optimistic (Year 2+ ceiling)All four layers activeYear 2 upside — requires operational milestones and attribution study
💡
T360 End-User Validation: Healthcare administrators confirmed that the ability to customize ROI inputs — such as one-way vs. round-trip rides, adherence scenario tiers (low, moderate, high), and patient activity types — is vital for aligning projected costs with institutional priorities and for justifying program adoption to internal finance personnel. The simulation's adjustable inputs directly reflect this finding.16
The Attribution Trap in Practice

Consider this scenario: You present the all-in estimate ($172K). Year 1 delivers $82K (FFS + TCM, no CCM enrollment yet, no VBC attribution study complete). What does the board experience?

📉
Perceived failure. The board anchored to $172K. $82K is 52% of expectation. Year 2 funding is threatened — even though $82K represents a 1.05× ROI and a genuinely successful FFS program. Anchoring error, not program failure, kills the Year 2 budget.
ROI-on-Net-Outlay Warning

Calculating ROI on net outlay (after the bank's $50K CRA contribution) inflates the apparent return by using the subsidized denominator. A finance committee skeptic will call this financial engineering. Present ROI on total economic investment.

Foundation 6 of 6 · Gateway

Readiness Check

Answer these five formative questions before entering the simulation. These are not scored — they confirm you have the key schema loaded before the CFO case begins. Review Foundation scenes if any feel uncertain.

1. The Shekelle 2022 meta-analysis found NEMT evidence for appointment adherence was rated at what level of certainty?
ALow — only observational studies included
BModerate — mixed evidence from RCTs and observational
CHIGH — appointment adherence evidence specifically
DVery High — confirmed by multiple large RCTs
The Shekelle meta-analysis rated the certainty of evidence for appointment adherence as HIGH. However, evidence on costs, health utilization, and clinical outcomes was rated LOW or VERY LOW. This asymmetry is the central tension in NEMT financial modeling — high confidence in behavior change, low confidence in downstream financial returns.
2. What is the approximate 2025 Medicare reimbursement rate for TCM code 99496 (high medical decision-making, face-to-face within 7 days)?

3. Under the AKS Local Transportation Safe Harbor (42 CFR § 1001.952(bb)), what is the distance limit for urban areas?
A10 miles
B25 miles
C50 miles
DNo limit in urban areas
Urban distance limit is ≤25 miles; rural is ≤75 miles (expanded from 50 miles in the 2020 final rule). Critically, the 2020 rule added a discharge exception: all distance limits are eliminated for patients being discharged from inpatient facilities to their residence. This makes TCM-enabling NEMT much easier to design compliantly — discharge rides home have no distance cap.
4. Which of the following correctly describes a compliant CCM/TCM billing architecture for the same patient in the same calendar month?
ABill both in the same month freely — they are complementary codes
BTCM period must end before month end; ≥20 min of CCM provided after; time not double-counted
CCount the TCM face-to-face visit time toward CCM's 20-minute threshold
DOnly one code can be billed per month; choose the higher-value code
CCM and TCM can be billed in the same month only when: (1) the 30-day TCM period ends before the calendar month ends, (2) at least 20 minutes of qualifying CCM services are provided after the TCM period, and (3) all CCM requirements are independently met. Time cannot be double-counted between codes. This creates a narrow but real window for dual billing when a patient is discharged early in the month.

Module 2 Key Takeaways
📐 Four-Layer Architecture

FFS → Coding → CCM/TCM → VBC. Each layer is additive; attribution confidence decreases as you move up. NEMT is an enabler within multi-component care models — not a standalone intervention.9

⚖️ Compliance Architecture

CPT requires AMA licensing. CCM/TCM time must be real, documented, and non-duplicative. Patient travel time is never billable. False Claims Act exposure is the primary legal risk.

📊 Board Presentation Discipline

Anchor to conservative-to-base (FFS + TCM). Present optimistic as Year 2 upside. Net outlay ROI is accurate but misleading. Three scenarios prevent single-number anchor failure.

🔬 Attribution Methodology

FFS and TCM: high-confidence, directly traceable. CCM: medium-confidence enrollment lift attribution. VBC: requires formal matched-cohort study. Label confidence explicitly in every board document.
